Dexter: A Smart Prosthetic Device for Transradial Amputees

Creating a smart below-the-elbow prosthesis that uses sensors and cameras to “feel” its environment and act intelligently. The project involves the design, fabrication, and testing of Dexter as an inexpensive, intuitive, and intelligent alternative to existing prosthetic hands.

Dexter will adjust its position, orientation and grip based on visual and force feedback to detect objects, determine appropriate grasps, move objects without crushing, dropping, or spilling the contents, and perform functions like opening doors, without the need for extensive user input. We call this new paradigm “assistive intelligence.” As the first intelligent anthropomorphic hand available for everyday use and advanced research applications alike, we ultimately expect that this device will bring about a paradigm shift in prosthetic design and engineering.

I have worked on Dexter for 8 months. I also have helped with the former iterations of this project.

I mostly worked on the electrical design and architecture and software development.

Spicio: An Automated Kitchen Assistant

Prototyped an automated voice controlled spice rack. Specifically wrote an Alexa skill for voice recognition, Javascript for sending HTTP POST requests to the Photon, and programing the Particle Photon using C++/C.

Competed in MakeMIT 2016 hardware hackathon and won 3rd overall place, Amazon Alexa award and an opportunity to pitch to Venture Capitalist in AlphaLab Gear competition

Spicio is voice controlled via Amazon Echo speaker. Echo recieves a voice command, transmits the information wirelessly to Spicio, and Spicio gives you spices

Servos control the spice dispensers, a motor with encoder makes the lazy susan spin and Particle Photon is in charge of all the control of this.

I worked on the embedded design on the Photon microcontroller, the communication between Echo and Photon and electronics.
